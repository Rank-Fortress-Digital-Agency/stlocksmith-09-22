Why should you never try to rekey or change your locks?


It may seem like a simple task to rekey or change your locks, but it is a dangerous job that should only be done by a professional. Here are some reasons why you should never try to do this yourself:

You could damage your door:


Trying to rekey or change your locks yourself can cause damage to your door. You could easily strip the screws or break the lock if you don’t know what you’re doing. A professional locksmith will learn how to change or rekey your locks without causing any damage.

You might also think you can save money by changing your locks, but this is not always the case. If you don’t know what you’re doing, you could spend more on repairs than you would have if you had just hired a professional in the first place.

In general, it’s always best to hire a professional locksmith when you need to change or rekey your locks. They have the experience and knowledge necessary to do the job right, and they can usually do it much faster than you could on your own. Not to mention, they’ll be able to provide you with a warranty on their work, so you can be sure that it’s done right.

You could void your warranty:


Many manufacturers will void the warranty on your door if you attempt to rekey or change the locks yourself. This means that if something goes wrong, you won’t be able to get it repaired for free. In addition, rekeying or changing your locks could damage the door, making it even more difficult and expensive to repair. Therefore, it’s always best to leave this job to a professional.

Contact a professional locksmith if you need to change or rekey your locks. They will have the tools and expertise necessary to do the job right without damaging your door or voiding your warranty.

It’s easy to make a mistake:


Even if you’re careful, it’s easy to make mistakes when rekeying or changing your locks. For example, you might accidentally reverse the pins’ order, rendering your lock unusable. If you make a mistake when changing your locks, the best thing to do is to call a professional locksmith. They will be able to help you fix your error and get your lock working again.

If you’re changing your locks because you’ve lost your keys, it’s essential to change the locks on all your home doors. This way, you can be sure that no one else has a key to your home. If you only change the lock on one door, anyone with a key to that door will still have access to your home.

When you’re ready to change your locks, ensure you have the right tools for the job. You’ll need a screwdriver and a new lock (or two if you’re changing more than one). If you’re unsure how to change a lock, plenty of guides and videos online can help.

Hiring a professional to rekey or change your locks is the best way to ensure that the job is done quickly and correctly. A professional will also be able to advise you on the best type of lock for your home based on your security needs.

You could get locked out:


If you make a mistake while rekeying or changing your locks, you could easily lock yourself out of your home. This would mean calling a locksmith to come and let you in, which can be costly and inconvenient. To avoid this, follow these simple tips.

When rekeying or changing your locks, double-check that the new keys fit before you throw away the old ones. It’s also a good idea to keep a spare key hidden outside your home in case you accidentally lock yourself out.
